Everything runs on a Raspberry Pi 5

The Pi is like the manager and Arduinos

Everything communicates over two USB

[music]
pH is an important factor for growing
algae, so I designed a whole module to
automate it.
pH is the measurement of how acidic or
alkaline the water is.
pH sensors that measure this
unfortunately kind of suck.
If they dry out, [music] they stop
working. So they have to be stored in a
special solution when not in use.
pH sensors also drift over time, which
means they lose accuracy [music] and
have to be recalibrated.
This is normally done by submerging them
in known value buffer solutions and
[music] adjusting the software.
For example, if the sensor is in a 7 pH
solution [music] but measures 7.5,
we basically tell the software that if
it reads 7.5 again, it really [music]
means 7.
Between any measurements, the sensor has
to be rinsed with deionized water
[music] to prevent cross-contamination.
We also have to keep the liquid sealed
when not in use, otherwise they will
evaporate or oxidize.
To keep the liquid sealed when not in
use, I combined the rotary motion and
the lid control into one mechanism.
Each lid travels inside a channel that
keeps the lid closed until they move to
the sensor.
Handling the deionized water rinse was a
little more tricky.
