The 2027 Horizon

Why 2027?

The countdown isn't a prediction. It's a reference point: the median year that AI researchers, forecasters, and lab insiders have converged on as a plausible window for human-level AI across most cognitive tasks. The actual date could be sooner. It could be later. What matters is that the smartest people working on this think it's within arm's reach, not decades away.

This is an illustrative countdown based on published expert forecasts as of 2025. It is not a scientific claim, a guarantee, or a prediction from the author. Expert predictions vary widely and are frequently wrong. The point is the direction and the pace, not the date.
01

The forecasting community has been updating fast

In 2022, AI Impacts surveyed hundreds of machine learning researchers. The median estimate for "high-level machine intelligence" was 2059. By 2023, that number had moved to 2047. By 2025, several prominent forecasters had moved their personal estimates into the late 2020s.

That's not a normal rate of update. When experts this deep in a field are revising their timelines by decades in a span of three years, it's worth paying attention.

Source: AI Impacts 2022 Expert Survey
02

The benchmark curve is steep and accelerating

GPT-2 (2019) couldn't write coherent paragraphs. GPT-3 (2020) could. GPT-4 (2023) passed the bar exam in the 90th percentile. Claude 3 (2024) outperformed most PhD students on graduate-level science questions. Each step took roughly one year.

If you plot capability against time and extrapolate even a conservative curve, the range where systems reach or exceed average human cognitive performance across most domains falls between 2026 and 2030. 2027 sits in the middle of that range.

Source: Sparks of AGI (Microsoft Research, 2023)
03

The labs are saying it themselves

OpenAI's Sam Altman has stated publicly that AGI may arrive within "a few years." Demis Hassabis at Google DeepMind has said it could be a decade at most. Dario Amodei at Anthropic put "powerful AI" in the 2026 to 2027 window in a widely-read 2024 essay.

These aren't speculators. These are the people building the systems, with visibility into capability curves that the public doesn't have. When they say soon, that's load-bearing information.

Source: Dario Amodei, "Machines of Loving Grace" (2024)
04

Compute is doubling faster than Moore's Law predicted

The amount of compute used to train frontier AI models has been doubling every 6 to 10 months since 2010. That's faster than historical semiconductor scaling. Data centers are now being built at the 1GW scale. The physical infrastructure for AGI-class training runs is already being constructed.

You don't build a gigawatt data center for something you think is 30 years away.

Source: Epoch AI Compute Trends

The honest answer

What if they're wrong?

Maybe it's 2029. Maybe it's 2032. Maybe a fundamental breakthrough takes longer than expected and the window is 2035.

That's still within the planning horizon of decisions you're making right now: where to live, what work to do, how to build income, what skills to develop, whether to take on a 30-year mortgage.

The book isn't built around a specific date. It's built around the fact that the change is coming faster than most people's plans account for, and that a higher quality of life is achievable on the other side if you start positioning now.

The countdown is a reminder. The preparation is the point.

The book walks through what to actually do with this information, in plain language, for regular people.
